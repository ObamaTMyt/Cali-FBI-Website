Chain of Command

Director - P. Lonn

As the 1st in command, the Director leads the entire FBI department and is responsible for the strategic oversight of the organization. The Director makes key executive decisions and ensures that all operations align with departmental goals and federal mandates. Additionally, the Director manages high-level relations with the DHS Department Coordination, reviews all departmental documentation, and provides guidance to the High Command team, fostering leadership and development at the highest level.

Deputy Director - G. Backstreet

As the 2nd in command, the Deputy Director plays a pivotal role in supporting the Director by managing daily FBI operations. This includes oversight of the Low Command and Supervisor teams, as well as ensuring that all personnel matters are handled efficiently, including the department's EUP (Employee Uniform Policy) and the Contractors Team. The Deputy Director also monitors audit logs and other documentation to ensure compliance and prevent abuse of permissions, acting as a safeguard for the integrity of department operations.

Assistant Deputy Director - P. Blart

This includes coordinating with subdivision leaders to ensure all activities are running smoothly, reviewing reports related to subdivision performance, and addressing any issues that may arise. In addition to managing subdivisions, the Assistant Deputy Director reviews and approves key documents before they reach the Director, ensuring accuracy and completeness in the decision-making process.

Executive Director - D. Saint

The Executive Director, 4th in command, leads the Internal Affairs division, playing a crucial role in maintaining ethical standards within the FBI. This position involves overseeing all internal investigations, managing the punishment logs, and ensuring that any misconduct within the department is dealt with appropriately. Additionally, the Executive Director supervises the command team, monitoring their behavior and ensuring alignment with departmental values and protocols.

Chief of Staff - Vacant

As the 5th in command, the Chief of Staff is responsible for the overall coordination of FBI operations. This role involves managing the Operation Coordinators and overseeing the planning and execution of joint departmental operations. The Chief of Staff ensures that all briefings, meetings, and transfers are conducted efficiently, contributing to the seamless execution of FBI operations across various units. Additionally, they act as a key point of communication within the department, ensuring clear and consistent messaging.
